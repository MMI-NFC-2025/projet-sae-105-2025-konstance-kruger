ALICE BALL

BIOGRAPHY

ALICE
AUGUSTA BALL

July 24, 1892 - December 31, 1916

Afro-American chemist

Known for developing one of the
most effective treatments against
leprosy

HER THEORY

During her studies at the University of Hawaii, Alice Ball became passionate about chemistry and was particularly interested in the structure of kava.

It was then that Dr. Harry T. Hollmann, assistant surgeon at Kalihi Hospital, called on her. He was looking to isolate the active principle of chaulmoogra oil, which was used at the time in the treatment of leprosy.

THE PROBLEM?

This oil has a particularly bitter taste and powerful side effects, which discouraged more than one patient.

Alice Ball then developed a revolutionary process: she managed to isolate the ethyl esters of the fatty acids from the oil, making them injectable and therefore much more effective and tolerable. Unfortunately, she died before being able to publish her results.

ITS
POPULARIZATION

After her disappearance, Arthur L. Dean (another chemist from the university) continued her work and launched large-scale production of the treatment.

In 1918, a Hawaiian doctor reported in the "Journal of the American Medical Association" that 78 patients were able to leave Kalihi Hospital after receiving these injections, a real turning point in the fight against leprosy.

Alice Ball is considered an emblematic example of the Matilda effect... Matilda because of the way her work was minimized and attributed to a man after her death, despite its scientific importance.

Decades later, researchers rediscovered her work and restored the truth, recognizing finally that Alice Ball was the true inventor of the process.
